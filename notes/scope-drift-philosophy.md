# Scope Drift
*An algorithmic philosophy for the hero field of joshgreenfield.com*

Scope Drift treats the vectorscope — the colorist's compass — as a landscape rather than an instrument. On a real scope, footage graded orange-and-teal collapses into two opposing lobes: warmth pulled one way, cool pulled the other, a whole film's palette reduced to two glowing petals on a dark circle. This movement inhabits that image. The algorithm does not *depict* footage; it behaves like the trace of footage that isn't there — a ghost signal, phosphor memory of a cut still being made.

The system lives in polar space. Particles are born into one of two lobes set 180 degrees apart, their angular positions scattered by a gaussian spread around each lobe's center, their radii breathing along seeded value-noise so the trace swells and thins like program audio. Nothing is placed; everything drifts. Each particle drags a short segment behind it — direction made visible — and the frame never fully clears: a translucent wash of the void color each tick lets history accumulate and decay, the way phosphor holds an image a half-second after the signal moves on.

The palette is a constraint, not a choice: grade-orange and grade-teal, blended in screen mode over near-black so the two families brighten where they cross but can never mud into brown. A rare white spark — two in a hundred — stands in for the specular highlight that clips the scope. The graticule itself is drawn at the threshold of visibility, redrawn each frame beneath the traces: rings, crosshair, the faint boxes of the six color targets. It is set dressing that tells you where you are without ever asking to be looked at.

The system listens. Scroll velocity feeds directly into segment length and brightness — shuttle fast through the page and the traces streak like footage scrubbed at 8x; settle, and they relax back to idle drift at a lazy thirty frames per second. This is the conceptual seed hidden in the mechanics: the page has a duration, the scroll bar is a playhead, and the scope reacts exactly as a scope does when an editor shuttles the transport. Those who have sat in a grading suite will feel it before they can name it.

Every run is seeded and reproducible — the same seed always conjures the same drift — and every parameter that shapes the field (density, drift rate, streak gain, lobe spread) is exposed for live exploration, because a meticulously crafted algorithm is one that survives being played with. The target is the mark of master-level implementation: motion so tuned it reads as atmosphere rather than animation, mathematics refined until it disappears into mood. If the field ever competes with the headline set over it, the parameters are wrong. The scope is the room tone of the page — present, breathing, and content to be background.
